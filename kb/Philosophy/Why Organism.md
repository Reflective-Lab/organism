# Why Organism

## The Problem

Converge answers: *"what actions are allowed to happen?"* It governs the commit boundary — proposals, promotion, authority, audit.

But it doesn't answer: *"how does an autonomous organization think, plan, and evolve?"*

That gap is Organism.

## What Organism Does

Organism translates human intent into governed, debated, simulated plans that can be submitted to Converge for execution. It is the reasoning layer between "I want X" and "here is a governed plan to achieve X."

The pipeline:
1. **Intent interpretation** — decompose human goals into machine specs
2. **Multi-model planning** — huddle loop with multiple reasoners
3. **Adversarial governance** — assumption breakers, constraint checkers, skeptics
4. **Simulation** — stress-test plans across outcome, cost, policy, causal, operational dimensions
5. **Organizational learning** — calibrate planning priors from execution outcomes

## What Organism Is Not

- Not a workflow engine (that's what it replaces)
- Not a chatbot (it plans, not chats)
- Not Converge (it has no authority — see [[Philosophy/Relationship to Converge]])
- Not a product (products sit on top — Wolfgang, Helms)
